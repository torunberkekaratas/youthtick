01
Erasmus+

The EU\'s flagship youth program

Erasmus+ is the European Union\'s program for education, training, youth and sport. With a budget of €26.2 billion for 2021–2027, it is the largest international mobility program in the world.

Türkiye is an Erasmus+ Programme Country, meaning Turkish youth organisations can apply for funding on equal terms with EU organisations.

Official Erasmus+ site
KA1 · Mobility
Youth Exchanges
Groups of 13–30-year-olds from different countries meet for 5–21 days to work on a shared topic. Typically 10–60 participants. Costs fully covered by Erasmus+ grant.
Age 13–30 5–21 days Group mobility

Training Courses for Youth Workers

KA2 · Partnerships
Cooperation Partnerships
Multi-year partnerships between 3+ organisations from different countries. Focus on developing practices, methods and materials around a shared youth issue. Budgets from €60K–€400K.

Small-Scale Partnerships
A simplified grant for organisations new to Erasmus+. Two or more partners, flexible project formats, maximum €30K budget. Ideal as a first Erasmus+ project.
2+ countries 6–24 months Entry-level

02
Solidarity Corps

European Solidarity Corps

The European Solidarity Corps (ESC) gives young people aged 18–30 the chance to volunteer, work or do traineeships with organisations across Europe — fully funded.

Turkish young people are eligible to participate, and Turkish organisations can host or send volunteers. ESC is separate from Erasmus+ but uses a similar framework.

Official ESC site
ESC · Volunteering
Individual Volunteering
Volunteer with an organisation in another European country for 2–12 months. Accommodation, meals, pocket money, travel and insurance are all covered. Ideal for a meaningful gap year or first international experience.
Age 18–30 2–12 months Fully funded
Learn more
ESC · Solidarity Projects
Solidarity Projects
Groups of 5+ young people from the same country organise and run their own local project to address a need in their community. No host organisation required — youth-led from the start.
Age 18–30 2–12 months Community-based
Learn more
ESC · Hosting
Hosting Volunteers in Türkiye
Accredited Turkish organisations can host European volunteers. This is one of the paths YouthTICK is exploring — bringing young Europeans to Yalova to contribute to local civic projects.
Accreditation needed For organisations In preparation

04
Germany–Türkiye

The Germany–Türkiye corridor

Germany has one of the deepest bilateral youth cooperation relationships with Türkiye. Dedicated programs and institutions fund youth exchanges, language learning and civic engagement across both countries.

This is YouthTICK\'s priority corridor — we are working to become a trusted bridge in this space.

JUGEND für Europa
German National Agency for Erasmus+
JUGEND für Europa is the German National Agency responsible for implementing Erasmus+ in the youth sector. They publish calls, offer training and connect organisations looking for international partners.
